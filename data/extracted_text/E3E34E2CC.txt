The Netherlands power origination team have concluded the
first
of the
so-called transmission deals with the above counterparty.
This
is
a sale and
buy back structure whereby Enron sells
on
a border basis to
a Dutch
distribution company who then sells back to Enron
a proportion of the power
on
a free
on Koppelnet basis.
The Koppelnet
is the Dutch grid.
The deals
are designed to secure
as much cross border capacity to take
advantage of the
still
significant
arbitrage between power prices within the
Netherlands
and those outside.
At the moment the grid operator,
TenneT,
allocates transmission capacity on
a pro
rata basis without
charge.
This has
led to significant gaming of the system with huge oversubscription for cross
border capacity,
and the structure we have developed in Enron has been
designed to take advantage of the system and still
stay within the
rules
laid
down by TenneT and the
regulator,
the DTe.
